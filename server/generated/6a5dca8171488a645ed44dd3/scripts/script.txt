[Scene 1] Earthquakes are powerful shakes caused by tectonic plates shifting, colliding, or sliding along underground fault lines.

[Scene 2] Underground, energy releases at the hypocenter. Directly above on the surface is the epicenter.

[Scene 3] A seismic sequence includes smaller foreshocks, the main earthquake, and weaker aftershocks that can last for years.

[Scene 4] Seismologists detect and record these underground ground vibrations using sensitive instruments called seismometers.

[Scene 5] Scientists once used the Richter scale, but now prefer the moment magnitude scale to measure total energy.

[Scene 6] Ground shaking is the primary hazard, directly destroying critical infrastructure like buildings, roads, and bridges.

[Scene 7] Shaking also triggers secondary disasters, including dangerous landslides, raging fires, and giant ocean tsunamis.

[Scene 8] Understanding earthquakes helps us prepare, build safer structures, and save lives during seismic events.
