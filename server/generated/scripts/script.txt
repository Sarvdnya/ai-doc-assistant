[Scene 1] The Himalayas span five countries, separating the Indian subcontinent plains from the high Tibetan Plateau.

[Scene 2] This massive range was uplifted by the violent tectonic collision of the Indian and Eurasian plates.

[Scene 3] It forms a vast twenty-four-hundred-kilometer arc, stretching from Nanga Parbat to Namcha Barwa.

[Scene 4] The range boasts Earth's highest peaks, including Mount Everest, with over one hundred exceeding seven thousand meters.

[Scene 5] The Indus-Yarlung suture zone separates the mountains from the adjacent, high-altitude Tibetan Plateau.

[Scene 6] These mountains feed major river systems, including the critical Indus, Ganges, and Tsangpo-Brahmaputra rivers.

[Scene 7] While fifty-three million people inhabit the mountains, six hundred million live within their river basins.

[Scene 8] Many peaks, like Mount Kailash, are sacred in Hinduism and Buddhism, keeping them off-limits to climbers.
