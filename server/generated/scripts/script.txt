[Scene 1] The Himalayas, spanning 2,400 kilometers, contain Earth's highest peaks, including the mighty Mount Everest.

[Scene 2] They formed millions of years ago when the Indian tectonic plate crashed into the Eurasian plate, uplifting the land.

[Scene 3] The range is anchored by Nanga Parbat in the west and Namcha Barwa in the far east.

[Scene 4] This massive range spans five nations: Nepal, India, China, Bhutan, and Pakistan, crossing complex borders.

[Scene 5] The glaciers feed major rivers like the Indus, Ganges, and Brahmaputra, vital for millions of people.

[Scene 6] While 53 million people live in the mountains, their river basins support over 600 million people.

[Scene 7] For Hindus and Buddhists, these mountains are sacred, with peaks like Mount Kailash remaining entirely off-limits to climbers.

[Scene 8] The Himalayas remain a powerful force, shaping Earth's geography, water systems, and diverse spiritual cultures.
