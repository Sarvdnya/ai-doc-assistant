[Scene 1] Earthquakes strike suddenly without warning, caused by shifting tectonic plates. These powerful events can disrupt infrastructure and destroy entire communities in seconds.

[Scene 2] Most damage and injuries occur due to poor building construction, unsafe locations, and a lack of preparation for securing heavy interior objects.

[Scene 3] Start by identifying the safest places in every room. Stay clear of windows, heavy furniture, fireplaces, and unsecured exterior walls.

[Scene 4] Build on stable bedrock and follow local anti-seismic building codes. Simple, symmetrical shapes and flexible materials help structures withstand intense shaking.

[Scene 5] Secure your home by fastening heavy bookcases to walls and locking cabinet doors. Even minor retrofits dramatically reduce building collapse risks.

[Scene 6] If shaking starts, drop to your knees, cover your head and neck, and hold on to a sturdy table. Do not try to run.

[Scene 7] If you are outdoors, find a clear area away from buildings, power lines, and trees. Drop to the ground and stay there.

[Scene 8] When the shaking stops, put on sturdy shoes and evacuate carefully. Expect aftershocks, which can cause further damage to weakened structures.

[Scene 9] Check for fires and immediately turn off gas lines. Never light matches or use electrical switches if you suspect a gas leak.

[Scene 10] Near coasts, move to higher ground immediately to escape potential tsunamis. In mountains, stay alert for falling rocks, landslides, or blocked streams.
